Grey Wolf

The gray wolf is one of the world's best-known and most-researched animals, with probably more books written about it than any other wildlife species.

  • Scientific Name: Canis Lupus
  • Average Weight: 43-45 kg
  • Average Lifespan: 5-6 years
  • Habitat: forests and mountains

The fear of wolves is pervasive in many human societies, the majority of recorded attacks on people have been attributed to animals suffering from rabies. Non-rabid wolves have attacked and killed people, mainly children, but this is rare, as wolves are relatively few, live away from people, and have developed a fear of humans from hunters and shepherds.
